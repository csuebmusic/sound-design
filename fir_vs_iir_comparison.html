Time-domain impulse response comparison.
x = sample number · y = amplitude · impulse fired at n=0 · FIR: delayed but shape-preserved · IIR: exponential decay with ringing
A single impulse (value 1, then all zeros) passes through both filters. FIR output: a clean, symmetric burst — all frequencies delayed by the same amount, transient shape preserved (linear phase). IIR output: an exponential decay that rings — different frequencies arrive at different times, smearing the attack (nonlinear phase). This smearing is exactly what makes IIR filters color drum transients differently from FIR.

Key tradeoffs:
FIR: always linear phase, always stable, sinc-shaped rolloff with nulls — computationally expensive for sharp cutoffs.
IIR: always nonlinear phase, risk of instability (|r| ≥ 1), smooth rolloff — very efficient. Rolloff: 1 pole = 20 dB/oct · 2 poles = 40 dB/oct · 4 poles = 80 dB/oct.
