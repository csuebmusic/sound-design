BiQuad Filter Explorer

The general second-order digital filter — combines feedforward (FIR) and feedback (IIR) in a single structure. Five coefficients give simultaneous control over poles and zeros. Adjust freely and observe the frequency response take shape.

y(n) = 1.00·x(n) + 0.00·x(n−1) + 0.00·x(n−2) − 0.00·y(n−1) − 0.00·y(n−2)

The BiQuad difference equation:
y(n) = g·x(n) + b1·x(n−1) + b2·x(n−2) − a1·y(n−1) − a2·y(n−2)
Feedforward (b) coefficients place zeros — frequencies where gain = 0. Feedback (a) coefficients place poles — frequencies where gain peaks. Every filter type (LPF, HPF, BPF, notch, shelving, peaking) is a specific configuration of these five values.
